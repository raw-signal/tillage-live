1. Who We Are

Tillage is a collaborative music platform for rough ideas — the voice memos, half-formed melodies, and unfinished sketches that would otherwise disappear. It exists to give those ideas a chance to grow through collaboration.

Tillage is not a record label, publishing house, or distribution platform. If you have a finished track you want to release commercially, there are better tools for that — DistroKid, Bandcamp, Spotify for Artists. Tillage is for the ideas that aren't there yet. Tillage is operated as an independent app ("we", "us", "our").

5. The Harvest

At the end of Tillage's first year, a curated selection of collaborative seeds may be pressed onto a limited physical vinyl record — the Harvest. Every contributor whose work appears will be credited by name in the liner notes and offered a discounted copy of the pressing.

The Harvest is not how Tillage makes money, and it is not how contributors should expect to make money. It is a permanent, physical document of what this community created together — the kind of thing most bedroom musicians never get to hold in their hands.

Any surplus from the pressing after costs goes to a worthy cause. Tillage does not profit from the Harvest.

Selection is editorial and limited by the running time of a vinyl record — only a small number of seeds will make the cut. If yours is selected, you'll be contacted directly and asked to confirm your participation before anything is pressed.

Tillage reserves the right to determine how Harvest content is presented and distributed. While contributors retain ownership of their individual recordings, the sealed collaborative work as a whole may only be licenced or distributed through Tillage — no individual contributor may act unilaterally on behalf of the collective work.

If you're planting a seed because you think it has serious commercial potential, that's great — but Tillage is not the right vehicle for that. Take it to a label, a publisher, or release it yourself. Plant it here because you want to see what it becomes when other people get their hands on it.
